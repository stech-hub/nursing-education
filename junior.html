Notes & Modules

Medical-Surgical Nursing: Care of adult patients with common medical and surgical conditions, patient assessment, care planning, and interventions.

Pharmacology: Basic drug classifications, administration routes, dosages, and side effects.

Community Health Nursing: Health promotion, disease prevention, home visits, immunization, and public health awareness.

Maternal & Child Health Nursing: Prenatal, antenatal, postnatal care, child growth, immunization, and nutrition.

Psychiatric Nursing (Intro): Mental health concepts, psychiatric disorders, therapeutic communication, and basic interventions.

Sample Q&A

Q1: What is medical-surgical nursing?

A: Nursing care of adults with medical conditions or undergoing surgery to restore health and prevent complications.

Q2: Define pharmacology.

A: Pharmacology is the study of drugs, their effects, uses, and administration in patient care.

Q3: What is the purpose of community health nursing?

A: To promote health, prevent disease, and improve the quality of life in communities.

Q4: Name two main areas in maternal & child health nursing.

A: Maternal care (pregnancy, childbirth) and child care (growth, nutrition, immunization).

Q5: Why is therapeutic communication important in psychiatric nursing?

A: It helps build trust, understand patient needs, and facilitate recovery in mental health care.
