Notes & Modules

Fundamentals of Nursing I: Covers the nursing process, roles of the nurse, basic care, patient assessment, infection control, and communication.

Anatomy & Physiology I: Study of body systems including skeletal, muscular, cardiovascular, respiratory, and nervous systems.

Basic Biology: Covers cell structure, tissues, basic life processes, and body homeostasis.

Medical Ethics: Principles of autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice, and confidentiality.

Communication Skills: Patient-nurse interaction, therapeutic communication, active listening, and report writing.

Procedures: Hand hygiene, vital signs measurement, patient positioning, bed making, bathing, oral care, injection techniques, first aid, emergency response.

100+ Exam Questions

Q1: What is nursing?

Nursing is the care of individuals to maintain or restore health, and to provide comfort.

Q2: List the steps of the nursing process.

Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation.

Q3: Define patient-centered care.

Care that respects and responds to the patient’s preferences, needs, and values.
