Notes

Fundamentals of Nursing I: Introduction to nursing, nursing process, basic patient care, hygiene, safety, and hospital rules.

Anatomy & Physiology I: Study of body systems: skeletal, muscular, nervous, and basic organ functions.

Basic Biology: Cells, tissues, body structure, and basic life processes.

Medical Ethics: Professional conduct, patient rights, confidentiality, and ethical nursing practices.

Communication Skills: Nurse-patient communication, report writing, listening skills, and empathy.

Sample Q&A

Q1: What is nursing?

A: Nursing is the care of individuals to maintain or recover health, and to provide comfort.

Q2: What are the main steps of the nursing process?

A: Assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation, and evaluation.

Q3: Name two major body systems studied in Anatomy I.

A: Skeletal system and muscular system.

Q4: What is the importance of medical ethics in nursing?

A: It guides nurses to provide care responsibly, respect patient rights, and maintain professionalism.

Q5: Why is communication important for nurses?

A: Effective communication improves patient care, ensures accurate reporting, and builds trust.
