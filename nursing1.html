Notes & Modules

Fundamentals of Nursing I: Introduction to nursing, nursing process, patient care basics, hygiene, safety, infection prevention, and ethics.

Anatomy & Physiology I: Skeletal, muscular, nervous systems, basic organ functions, and homeostasis.

Basic Biology: Cells, tissues, organ systems, and life processes.

Medical Ethics: Professional conduct, patient rights, confidentiality, ethical practices.

Communication Skills: Therapeutic communication, patient interaction, report writing.

Procedures: Hand hygiene, bed making, vital signs, patient positioning, bathing, oral care, injections, first aid, safety measures.

100+ Exam Questions

Q1: What is nursing?

Care of individuals to maintain or restore health and provide comfort.

Q2: List the steps of the nursing process.

Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation.

Q3: Define patient-centered care.

Care that respects and responds to patient’s preferences, needs, and values.

Q4: Name two major body systems studied in Anatomy I.

Skeletal and muscular systems.

Q5: Why is communication important in nursing?

Ensures accurate reporting, improves patient care, and builds trust.
