Notes & Modules

Advanced Medical-Surgical Nursing: Management of complex illnesses, surgical care, ICU monitoring, emergency interventions, and end-of-life care.

Pharmacology III: Advanced drug therapy, critical medications, high-risk patients, and complex dosage calculations.

Critical Care & ICU Nursing: Advanced monitoring, ventilator care, emergency procedures, shock management, and life support basics.

Pediatric Nursing III: Complex pediatric care, chronic conditions, advanced growth monitoring, and emergency pediatric interventions.

Maternal & Child Health III: High-risk pregnancies, labor & delivery complications, postpartum care, neonatal emergencies.

Community & Public Health Nursing II: Epidemiology, health promotion programs, community outreach, disaster management.

Procedures: ICU care, advanced IV therapy, tracheostomy, ventilator monitoring, cardiac care, emergency protocols, patient education.

100+ Exam Questions

Q1: What is the focus of Advanced Medical-Surgical Nursing?

Care of patients with complex medical and surgical conditions, including ICU monitoring and emergencies.

Q2: Name two critical care interventions for ICU patients.

Ventilator management and advanced hemodynamic monitoring.

Q3: Why is leadership important in senior nursing?

Ensures efficient team coordination, quality patient care, and policy adherence.

Q4: What are key components of community health programs?

Health promotion, disease prevention, epidemiology, and public education.

Q5: Why is research essential at senior level?

Supports evidence-based practice, improves outcomes, and guides policy decisions.
