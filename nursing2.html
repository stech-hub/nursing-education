Notes & Modules

Fundamentals of Nursing II: Expanded nursing process, patient-centered care, advanced assessment, infection control, and professional ethics.

Anatomy & Physiology II: Cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive, endocrine, urinary, and reproductive systems.

Microbiology: Pathogens, bacteria, viruses, fungi, sterilization, disinfection, and infection prevention.

Nutrition: Food nutrients, dietary needs, malnutrition, diet planning for patients.

Psychology: Human behavior, stress management, patient emotional support, therapeutic communication.

Procedures: Vital signs, injections (IM, SC, ID), IV therapy, wound care, patient positioning, hygiene care, feeding, first aid.

100+ Exam Questions

Q1: What is the main focus of Fundamentals of Nursing II?

Advanced patient care, assessment, interventions, and professional ethical practices.

Q2: Name the major systems studied in Anatomy & Physiology II.

Cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive, endocrine, urinary, and reproductive systems.

Q3: What is the importance of microbiology in nursing?

It helps nurses understand pathogens, infection prevention, and control measures.

Q4: List three types of injections used in clinical practice.

Intramuscular (IM), Subcutaneous (SC), and Intradermal (ID) injections.

Q5: Why is patient nutrition important in nursing?

Proper nutrition supports healing, maintains health, and improves overall patient outcomes.
