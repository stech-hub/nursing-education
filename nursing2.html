Notes & Modules

Fundamentals of Nursing II: Patient care techniques, vital signs, bed making, hygiene, infection control, patient safety, nursing documentation.

Anatomy & Physiology II: Cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive, urinary, reproductive, endocrine and lymphatic systems.

Microbiology: Types of microorganisms, infection transmission, sterilization, disinfection, immunity, hospital-acquired infections.

Nutrition: Nutrients, balanced diet, malnutrition, therapeutic diets, nutrition for special conditions (pregnancy, childhood, illness).

Psychology: Human behavior, growth and development, learning, memory, emotions, stress and coping, personality, nurse–patient relationship.

Sample Q&A

Q1: What are vital signs?

A: Measurements of essential body functions: temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure.

Q2: Name the steps of the nursing process.

A: Assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation, and evaluation.

Q3: What is sterilization?

A: A process that kills all microorganisms on instruments or surfaces to prevent infection.

Q4: Give two major functions of the respiratory system.

A: Gas exchange (oxygen in, carbon dioxide out) and maintaining blood pH balance.

Q5: Why is nutrition important in nursing care?

A: Proper nutrition supports healing, energy, immunity, and overall patient recovery.
