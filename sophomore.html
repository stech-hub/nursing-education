Notes & Modules

Medical-Surgical Nursing I: Study of common diseases, patient care management, pre/post-operative care, and clinical decision-making.

Pharmacology I: Basic pharmacokinetics, drug classifications, administration, dosage calculations, side effects, and patient monitoring.

Pathophysiology: Disease mechanisms, etiology, clinical manifestations, and nursing interventions.

Pediatric Nursing I: Growth and development, common pediatric illnesses, patient care, family education, and safety.

Maternal & Child Health I: Antenatal care, labor and delivery, postpartum care, newborn care, and maternal education.

Procedures: IV therapy, injections, vital signs, wound care, catheterization, hygiene care, patient positioning, feeding, first aid.

100+ Exam Questions

Q1: What is Medical-Surgical Nursing I focused on?

Patient care management of common diseases, pre/post-operative care, and clinical decision-making.

Q2: Define pharmacokinetics.

The study of how the body absorbs, distributes, metabolizes, and excretes drugs.

Q3: Name two common pediatric nursing principles.

Growth/development monitoring and family-centered care.

Q4: List three maternal & child health nursing responsibilities.

Antenatal care, labor & delivery support, postpartum care.

Q5: Why is infection control important in clinical practice?

Prevents spread of disease, protects patients and staff, and ensures safe care.
